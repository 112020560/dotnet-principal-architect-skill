# Engineering Assessment Framework (EAF)

> A vendor-neutral engineering governance framework that transforms technical evidence into executive decisions.

---

# Project Vision

The Engineering Assessment Framework (EAF) aims to become a comprehensive and standardized framework for evaluating software systems, measuring engineering maturity, supporting executive decision-making, and certifying production readiness.

The framework is designed to be technology agnostic, vendor neutral, repeatable, and extensible.

Rather than producing isolated reports or subjective opinions, the framework establishes a structured engineering lifecycle that converts technical observations into measurable engineering outcomes and executive decisions.

---

# Why This Project Exists

Modern software organizations often struggle with inconsistent engineering evaluations.

Architecture reviews vary between architects.

Security assessments depend on individual experience.

Production readiness is frequently subjective.

Technical debt is difficult to communicate to business stakeholders.

Engineering decisions are often made without a standardized methodology.

The Engineering Assessment Framework exists to solve these problems by providing a consistent engineering model that can be applied across projects, organizations, and technology stacks.

---

# The Core Idea

The framework follows a progressive engineering lifecycle.

```text
Software System
        │
        ▼
Engineering Reviews
        │
        ▼
Engineering Scorecards
        │
        ▼
Executive Assessments
        │
        ▼
Engineering Certifications
        │
        ▼
Engineering Governance
```

Each layer adds value.

No layer duplicates the work of another.

---

# Engineering Philosophy

The framework is based on four simple principles.

## Reviews produce evidence.

Reviews inspect software systems and identify engineering findings.

Reviews answer:

> What did we find?

---

## Scorecards summarize engineering health.

Scorecards consolidate engineering evidence into measurable capabilities.

Scorecards answer:

> How healthy is this engineering domain?

---

## Assessments support executive decisions.

Assessments transform engineering evidence into business and engineering decisions.

Assessments answer:

> What decision should be made?

---

## Certifications formalize decisions.

Certifications acknowledge that predefined engineering criteria have been satisfied.

Certifications answer:

> Has the organization formally approved this outcome?

---

# What Makes This Framework Different

The Engineering Assessment Framework is not a checklist.

It is not a maturity model.

It is not a compliance standard.

It is not an architecture review template.

Instead, it defines a complete engineering governance lifecycle that transforms technical evidence into executive decision-making.
