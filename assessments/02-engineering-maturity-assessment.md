# Engineering Maturity Assessment

> Transform consolidated engineering evidence from all domain scorecards into a formal evaluation of the organization's engineering maturity and long-term sustainability.

---

# Purpose

The Engineering Maturity Assessment determines the overall engineering maturity of a software organization.

Its objectives are to:

- Consolidate maturity evidence from all domain scorecards.
- Evaluate the repeatability and consistency of engineering practices.
- Identify maturity gaps across engineering capabilities.
- Assess long-term engineering sustainability.
- Prioritize organizational engineering investments.
- Produce a formal engineering maturity determination.

This assessment does not perform engineering reviews.

This assessment does not recalculate scorecard scores.

All engineering evidence originates from the domain scorecards produced by the Engineering Review Framework.

# Engineering Maturity Model

The Engineering Assessment Framework uses the following maturity levels.

| Level | Name | Characteristics |
|-------|------|-----------------|
| 1 | Initial | Ad hoc practices. Dependent on individual contributors. Inconsistent outcomes. |
| 2 | Developing | Emerging practices. Partially documented. Inconsistently applied. |
| 3 | Managed | Defined processes. Consistent application. Measurable outcomes. |
| 4 | Mature | Optimized practices. Proactive governance. Continuous improvement. |
| 5 | Engineering Excellence | Systematic engineering. Institutional knowledge. Industry benchmark. |
